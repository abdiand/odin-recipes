Brandon's Smoked Brisket

What is Smoked Brisket?

A huge chunk of cow that comes off a smoker very deep in a ebony color.It is not burnt, and beneath the crust is the usually the most juicy, tender meat full of flavor.

Ingredient List

  • Sea Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Chipotle Powder
  • Olive Oil

    • Please feel free to just use salt and pepper if that is all you want!

    Recipe Instructions

    1. Trim It

      2. Tidy up the brisket by trimming the fat cap to leave about ¼" fat layer and removing the silver skin from the underside.

    2. Season It

      3. Generously with a 50:50 ratio of kosher salt and ground black pepper. (or freshly ground pepper.)

      Let it sit out at room temperature for about an hour.

    3. Prep Your Smoker

      4. This is one of the most important key parts in making sure your brisket comes out great, Brandon recommends setting up the temperature between 250°F to 275°F with post oak.

      (or your preferred type of wood)

    4. Smoke It

      5. Place the brisket in the smoker and cook it with the lid closed.

    5. Wrap It

      6. With butcher paper when the bark is formed and the internal temperature reaches about 165° F. Then place back in the smoker until the internal temperature of the flat (thinner side) reaches between 200°F and 205° F, and it feels very tender and flexible to the touch.

    6. Rest It

      7. Still wrapped for at least one to two hours before slicing.

    7. Enjoy!
